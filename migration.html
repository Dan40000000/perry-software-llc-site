The 4-Week Playbook

Here's Exactly What Happens

You get a dedicated migration specialist from day one. Here's the step-by-step — no surprises, no gaps, no "figure it out yourself."

WK1

Week One

We Learn Everything About You

  • Discovery call — we map your current system, workflows, and every quirk your team relies on
  • Data audit — we catalog every patient, note, billing record, and document in your old EHR
  • Custom migration plan built and sent to you within 48 hours — you know exactly what's happening and when
  • Your team does exactly zero work during this phase
WK2

Week Two

We Extract and Rebuild Your World

  • Full data extraction from your current EHR — every patient record, clinical note, billing history, and appointment pulled and verified
  • Data cleaning and mapping — we normalize and import everything into Perry exactly as it should appear
  • System configuration — your templates, macros, note types, and workflows built to match how your practice actually runs
  • Integration wiring — labs, pharmacy, clearinghouse — all connected before go-live
WK3

Week Three

We Train Your Team Until They're Confident

  • On-site or remote training — every person on your team, not just the admin. Providers, front desk, billers, everyone
  • Parallel testing — your team uses Perry with real patient data while still running your old system as a safety net
  • Live customizations — any changes, tweaks, or preferences identified during training get made on the spot
  • Dry run go-live — we simulate Monday morning so there are zero surprises
WK4

Week Four — The Final Push

You Close Out. We Take It From Here.

  • Monday–Thursday: Final data sync, verification checks, and approval sign-offs
  • Friday: You see your last patient in your old system. You log out. That's your only job.
  • Friday evening → Monday morning: We do the final migration, run verification, and prepare your Perry system for day one
  • Monday 8:00 AM: You open Perry. Every patient is there. Every note. Every schedule. Your team walks in and gets to work.
